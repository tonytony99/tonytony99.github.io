P(Ci) = probability the car is behind door i

P(Ci|Dj) = probability the car is behind door i given door j is opened

P(A|B) = P(B|A) P(A)/P(B) (i.e. Baye's Theorem)

Each door's equally likely to have the car, P(C1)=P(C2)=P(C3)=1/3

Suppose you choose door 1, and then door 2 is opened ...

P(C1|D2) = P(D2|C1) P(C1)/ P(D2) = 1/2 x 1/3 / 1/2 = 1/3

P(C2|D2) = 0

P(C3|D2) = P(D2|C3) P(C3)/ P(D2) = 1 x 1/3 / 1/2 = 2/3

Since P(C3|D2) > P(C1|D2) we should swap from door 1 to door 3.
